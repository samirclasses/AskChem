Molar conductivity (Λm) of an electrolyte is the conducting power of all the ions produced by one mole of it in a solution placed between two large electrodes one centimetre apart. The molar conductivity of a solution at any concentration is related to its conductivity (k) by the relation Cm × Λm = k × 1000. Molar conductivity of the electrolyte is highest at infinite dilution when each ion of the electrolyte makes a definite contribution towards the molar conductivity. Molar conductivity at infinite dilution is called limiting molar conductivity (Λ°m). The degree of dissociation of electrolyte at any concentration can be obtained from the ratio of Λm/Λ°m.
